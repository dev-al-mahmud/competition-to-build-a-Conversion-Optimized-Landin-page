The Industry Problem

The Way Trade Purchasing Has Always Worked

For decades, trade pricing has operated on a simple and consistent principle: the more you buy, the better your rate. Large national operators with significant purchasing volumes access pricing tiers that independent contractors simply cannot reach on their own.

This isn't unfair it is the natural logic of wholesale distribution. But it has always meant that the independent electrician, the sole-trader HVAC business, and the small solar installation company pay more for the same products, from the same suppliers, simply because of scale. The system rewards volume. And independent contractors, by definition, operate without it.

The Founding Insight

Where This Insight Came From

The team behind Affiliate Warehouse didn't discover this problem through research or consulting reports. They lived it. With more than 25 years of experience operating in the trade industry and through building and running Aussie Antennas and Electrical they understood from the inside exactly how purchasing decisions are made, how pricing tiers function, and where independent contractors consistently lose ground to larger competitors.

It was not a theoretical gap. It was a daily reality — visible in every purchase order, every supplier negotiation, and every invoice comparison against a national operator. And it raised a straightforward question: what would happen if independent contractors could coordinate their purchasing volume, without giving up their existing supplier relationships, individual account traceability, or the wholesale systems they already relied on?

The Solution

The Simple Idea That Followed

The answer became Affiliate Warehouse. Not a buying group, not a reseller, and not a model that bypasses or undermines supplier relationships. Instead, a coordinated procurement structure that aggregates the purchasing volume of approved preferred contractors nationally allowing the network to access pricing structures typically reserved for large corporate operators, while every contractor continues to purchase through their own existing wholesale channels.

The concept is straightforward: the collective buying power of many, applied with the transparency and traceability that wholesalers expect. Each contractor retains their own account. Every purchase remains job-coded and fully traceable. Nothing about the way wholesalers operate is changed or circumvented.

The Build

A System That Matches the Scale of the Work

Delivering on that idea required more than a concept. It required establishing formal relationships with national wholesale networks TradeZone, Ideal Electrical, and Voltex and developing a system that could track, compare, and verify pricing across individual purchases without disrupting the way contractors actually work on the ground.

Every purchase moves through normal wholesaler systems. No goods are resold. No supplier terms are bypassed or altered. Affiliate Warehouse operates entirely within the existing wholesale infrastructure adding a layer of collective purchasing power without changing the mechanics that wholesalers and contractors already depend on.

The Foundation

Built on 25+ Years of Contractor Relationships

The credibility of Affiliate Warehouse is built on relationships, not software. Over 25 years of operating in the trade industry, the founding team developed deep, direct relationships with contractors, suppliers, and industry partners across Australia. These relationships form the foundation of the preferred contractor network and they are the reason the model works in practice, not just in principle.

Approval is never automatic. Every preferred contractor is individually reviewed and assessed. That standard exists because the model depends on contractors who understand it, operate within it with integrity, and contribute to the collective purchasing volume that benefits the entire network.

The founding experience is not simply context it is the reason the model exists. Affiliate Warehouse was built to solve a problem the founding team experienced directly, by people who understood the industry well enough to design something that works within it properly.

Fee Structure — Explained

How the Fee Structure Works —
Explained Simply.

The logic behind how Affiliate Warehouse charges, why it was designed this way, and what it means for your business in practice.

The Rationale

Why the Fee Exists

Affiliate Warehouse generates revenue in exactly one way: by retaining a small percentage of the verified savings it delivers to contractors. There are no subscriptions, no access costs, and no charges based on order volume or time period. If no saving is achieved on a purchase, nothing is charged for that purchase, or for any period where savings do not occur.

This structure was chosen deliberately. A fee that only applies to verified, confirmed savings means that Affiliate Warehouse's commercial interests are permanently aligned with the contractors it serves. The business only grows when it consistently delivers measurable outcomes.

The Mechanics

How the Fee is Applied

When a purchase is made through Affiliate Warehouse and a discount is verified against the contractor's standard trade pricing, Affiliate Warehouse retains 1.1% of that verified saving. The remainder the full balance of the discount is retained by the contractor. The fee is calculated monthly, applied only to confirmed savings, and is never estimated, approximated, or charged in advance.

Example: A contractor achieves a $1,000 verified discount on a purchase. Affiliate Warehouse retains $11 — 1.1% of the saving. The contractor keeps $989. The fee is always proportional, always calculated from the verified figure, and always transparent.
The Outcome for Contractors

How Contractors Benefit

The net result for approved preferred contractors is access to improved pricing structures without any fixed cost overhead. Because the fee is proportional to savings achieved — and zero when no savings are achieved there is no scenario in which a contractor loses money through participation. The risk is asymmetric in the contractor's favour: potential savings on one side, no financial downside on the other.

Combined with the price tracking system and side-by-side comparison tools available to approved preferred contractors, the model provides trade businesses with consistent, ongoing visibility over their purchasing position.

The Design Principle

Transparent and Simple

The fee structure was designed to be explained in a single conversation. There are no tiers, no thresholds, no volume requirements, and no complex conditions. If a saving is verified, 1.1% is retained by Affiliate Warehouse. If no saving is achieved, nothing is charged. Billing occurs monthly. Participation is non-exclusive and can be ended at any time with no exit fees or penalties of any kind.

This simplicity is intentional. A fee structure that requires a spreadsheet to understand creates doubt where there should be clarity. The model is designed to be understood immediately and it is.
